## Critical gap: #51 is not fully wired

**What the overnight built:**
- `seed_lor_crosswalk_2006_to_2021.csv` (448 rows, all weight sums 0.999-1.000, tolerance OK)
- `int_berlin_ewr_plr2021` — EWR data with pre-2021 rows reapportioned to 2021 PLR codes

**What is missing:**
`fct_gentrification_change` still shows 0 non-null `gentrification_delta` rows at 2021.
The LAG window is partitioned by `(area_code, area_vintage)`. For the 2020->2021 boundary:
- 2020 rows: area_code = pre-2021 PLR code, area_vintage='lor_pre2021'
- 2021 rows: area_code = 2021 PLR code, area_vintage='lor_2021'
Different codes, different vintage — LAG produces NULL.

**What a full fix requires:**
Option 1 (recommended): Build a unified time-series model that maps ALL years to 2021 PLR codes.
This requires applying the crosswalk to the POI side too (int_osm_poi_plr → remap to 2021 codes
for years <= 2020) so that both POI and EWR share the same area_code scheme. Then `int_gentrification_ts`
can join on area_code alone and `fct_gentrification_change` LAG will see matching codes across 2020-2021.

Option 2 (simpler, less clean): In `fct_gentrification_change`, use the crosswalk to match
pre-2021 PLR codes to their 2021 equivalents and compute a weighted-average delta across the
vintage break. This is more complex in SQL and less transparent.

**Recommendation:** Option 1. Add a new issue for this. The crosswalk seed and int_berlin_ewr_plr2021
are already correct foundations. The extra step is mapping int_osm_poi_plr area_codes to 2021 codes
for years <= 2020.

**Impact of not fixing it now:** The time series is correct within each vintage (2008-2020, 2021-2026)
but has a gap at the boundary. C6 temporal methodology validation will note this as a known limitation.

## E1 findings note

Rho values are -0.19 to -0.33, all p<0.0001. This is **a real finding**, not a bug:
- High POI dynamism = new businesses opening in lower-status areas (pre-gentrification signal)
- The 2018 thesis detected this as a stage, not a steady state
- The negative correlation is methodologically coherent: areas currently gentrifying have high
